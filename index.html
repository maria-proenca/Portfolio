And (Now and Then) the Mirror Cracked:

O design entre o feminino, as margens e a ficção

2021/ 2022 Graduation show
Identity Design, Editorial Design, Webdesign with Duarte Costa, Raquel Gomes and Rodrigo Amâncio

At the end of another cycle of study, the project And (Now and Then) the Mirror Cracked emerges as a space of convergence — a place that carries the academically developed narratives of graduating students, their intersections, the discourse that arises from them, alongside the voices that sparked them, in an exchange that is never truly complete.

In parallel with the narratives created, And (Now and Then) the Mirror Cracked seeks to (re)vision design and the discourse emerging from its logics through a feminist lens, a process that itself seeks to generate counter-discourse and subvert narratives. The lens of the feminine can be understood as a counter-history, a transgression of dominant narratives and a reconfiguration of the canon. It is a rupture that takes us back to the project's title, which derives from Anneke Smelik's «And The Mirror Cracked» (1998), and to which we add a parenthesis that binds us to reality and denounces its volatility.

It is by challenging the constraints of the counter-narrative and making it broader that we make room for the alternative, for dreams and convictions that challenge the hegemonic discourse finally break the mirror—this is where And (Now and Then) the Mirror Cracked positions design.

DEVIANT:

The Night as A Place

Academic Project
Editorial Design, Video installation with Duarte Costa and Rodrigo Amâncio

DEVIANT is a magazine born from the need to create a dialogue that reveals empowerment, —providing a space of freedom and a refuge of (self)-expression for those who are marginalized in the 21st century. Each issue derives from the experience in a significant space seeking to establish ramifications with other subjects and events of the same nature.

The first edition, «The Night as A Place» is dedicated to the Club, identity, music and the way in which, in the late 20th century, these nocturnal spaces served as a safe space for those who yearned to be themselves.
